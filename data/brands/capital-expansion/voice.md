# Capital Expansion — voice

The brand is a peer talking to an operator, not a marketer talking to a lead.

## Register

- Plain. Sentences are short and load-bearing.
- Direct. The reader's situation is the subject, not us.
- Quietly confident. We know who funds what; we don't need to brag.
- Operator-to-operator. The reader runs a real business; so do we (or so we behave).

## Reusable phrases (copywriter should keep using these)

- "The right capital finds the right operator."
- "We know who funds what."
- "From factoring to SBA to equipment finance — we know who funds what."
- "Tell us your situation."
- "We make the match."
- "Warm intros, not cold lists."
- "No origination conflicts."
- "Operator-first."
- "Across the spectrum."
- "Across capital types."
- "We protect your time and our partners' capital."
- "If we don't have a fit, we'll say so."
- "Capital is fragmented."
- "You shouldn't have to figure out who funds what."

## Words and framings to avoid

- "Solutions" (empty noun)
- "Best-in-class," "world-class," "industry-leading"
- "Innovative," "cutting-edge"
- "Disrupting" anything
- "Empower," "unlock potential"
- "Frictionless," "seamless," "magical"
- "Synergy," "leverage" (as a verb)
- "Concierge" — too consumer-luxury for SMB operators
- "Reach out" — say "tell us" or "send us a note"
- Em-dashes used as a stylistic crutch (one per paragraph max)
- Exclamation points
- Emojis in body copy

## Sentence shapes that work

- "If you're [specific situation], you probably already know [specific friction]."
- "[Capital type] is for operators who [specific use]. It is not for [common mismatch]."
- "We're not a lender. [Concrete consequence of that.]"
- "Most marketplaces [common pattern]. We don't [specific contrast]."

## Tone calibration: the line we don't cross

Confident-not-cocky. Specific-not-clever. Direct-not-blunt. We are not the smartest person in the room; we are the person who has already done this for hundreds of operators in your shoes (without claiming a count we cannot prove — see proof-and-credibility.md).

## Voice in different surfaces

- **Direct mail headline**: 6–10 words, declarative, names the reader's situation. Example: "Factoring rates dropped this quarter. Most carriers are still on last year's contract."
- **Direct mail body**: 60–120 words, peer-to-peer, ends with one specific next step.
- **Email subject line**: 4–8 words, no clickbait, names the situation or the partner type. Example: "Equipment finance for two new dry vans."
- **Email body**: short paragraphs, 80–150 words total, one ask.
- **Landing page hero**: declarative sentence pair, no jargon.
- **Voice agent**: "Hi, this is [name] with Capital Expansion. We sent you a postcard about [situation]. Got a minute to talk through what you're trying to fund?"
