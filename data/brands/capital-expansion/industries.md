# Capital Expansion — industries served

Industries the brand currently serves, with the canonical capital fit per industry. Use this to route per-recipient creative when the recipient's industry is known (which is always the case when the audience is sliced from data-engine-x).

| Industry | Primary capital fit | Secondary fit | Operator pain language |
|---|---|---|---|
| Trucking & freight | Factoring | Equipment finance | "Cash strangled by net-60 broker terms; need a tractor I can't put 25% down on" |
| Manufacturing | Equipment finance | ABL, SBA (acq.) | "Machinery purchase or sale-leaseback to free liquidity for a contract ramp" |
| Staffing & PEO | Factoring | LOC | "Clients pay net-45; payroll is weekly" |
| Wholesale & distribution | ABL | Factoring | "Profitable on paper, broke on cash; inventory build for peak season" |
| Professional services | LOC | SBA (acq./buyout) | "Smooth retainer/project cycles; partner buy-in" |
| Restaurants & hospitality | Equipment finance | LOC, SBA | "Equipment refresh; second-location build-out" |
| Healthcare practices | Equipment finance | SBA (acq./buy-in) | "Imaging/dental chairs; senior partner buyout" |
| Government contractors | Factoring | LOC, ABL | "Mobilization capital between award and first invoice" |

## How to use this in creative

For per-recipient creative, the audience spec already locks the industry. The copywriter consults this table to pick the primary capital-type framing for the piece. The capital-types.md file then gives the operator-language examples to weave in.

A recipient is a real specific operator with industry, size, and (often) equipment/AR/season-stage signal in the spec attributes. Use that signal. Avoid generic industry framings when the data lets you be specific to the recipient.

## Industries we explicitly do not serve (yet)

- Real estate development / non-owner-occupied CRE — different financing model; we do not route here.
- Pre-revenue startups — different capital ladder (equity, SAFE, etc.); we do not route here.
- Cannabis — regulatory specialty market; we do not route here.
- Pure consumer retail (without B2B AR component) — limited factoring/ABL fit; case-by-case only.

If a recipient appears to fit one of these excluded categories, the piece should not be sent. Audience spec filters should already exclude these upstream; this is a backstop note for the copywriter.
