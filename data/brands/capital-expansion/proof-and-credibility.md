# Capital Expansion — proof & credibility

This is a new brand. There is no track record yet. The copywriter must respect that and not fabricate.

## What we can claim

- We work with capital partners across factoring, SBA, equipment finance, ABL, LOC, and RBF.
- We do not earn on the spread.
- We see the operator's situation before recommending a partner.
- We send warm intros with context, not cold lists.
- We focus on U.S. SMB operators in the $1M–$50M revenue band.
- Industry coverage as listed in industries.md.

## What we cannot yet claim

We do NOT have a track record yet. Until we do, the following claims are off-limits:

- "We've matched [N] operators with capital partners." (Pick a number when we have one.)
- "Operators close [X]% of warm intros." (Need the data.)
- Customer counts, dollar volumes, time-to-close averages, retention figures — none of these.
- Any specific named customer testimonial or case study. We have none.
- Specific partner names ("we work with [Bank X]"). Partner relationships are private until disclosed by mutual agreement.

## What to do instead of fabricating proof

The brand-new status is fine. The strategic-direction doc is explicit on this:

> Brand age / patina is not relevant: a brand-new brand is fine, and Ben can openly say the brand is new. What is relevant is targeting precision and routing — that is what the partner is paying for.

Acceptable substitutes for missing track record:

- **Be specific instead of impressive.** Specific operator situations + specific capital types + specific partner archetypes are more persuasive than vague volume claims.
- **Lead with the matching logic, not the matching count.** "If you're a freight broker on net-60 terms with a $4M run rate, you don't want a bank LOC. You want a transportation factor with same-day funding." That sentence demonstrates expertise more than "we've helped 500 brokers."
- **Show the work.** Identifying the right capital type for a recipient's specific situation is itself credibility. The piece's recommendation IS the proof.

## When the brand has a track record (later)

Once Capital Expansion has run live initiatives for 90+ days and produced verifiable outcomes, this file gets updated with concrete numbers. Until then, every claim must pass the test: "Could a partner read this and call us out on it?"

If the answer is "they could," do not write it.
